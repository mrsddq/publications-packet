# Research Statement

My current portfolio sits at the intersection of computer vision, applied ML engineering, and MLOps. The central theme is not only building models, but building the surrounding evidence: reproducible configs, realistic evaluation protocols, failure analysis, and deployment-aware documentation.

The highest-value direction is medical and safety-sensitive vision: segmentation, robustness, explainability, annotation quality, and document understanding. These areas reward careful experimentation more than flashy demos, so the portfolio is structured around honest limitations, ablation plans, and reproducibility notes.

The next research milestone is to convert structured repositories into measured studies with public datasets, reported metrics, qualitative examples, and concise write-ups.
